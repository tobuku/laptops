Verdict

Choose the ASUS ROG Strix G16 if: You want the most powerful GPU in this price tier, you prioritize the best possible display quality, or you want the aggressive ROG aesthetic with full RGB control. The RTX 4070 Ti Super delivers noticeably better 4K gaming and ray tracing performance.

Choose the Lenovo Legion 5i Pro if: You want excellent gaming performance at $200 less — because in most games at 1440p, the RTX 4070 in the Legion 5i Pro performs within 10–15% of the RTX 4070 Ti Super. That's $200 you can put toward more RAM, a gaming headset, or a better monitor.

Bottom line: The Legion 5i Pro wins on value. The ROG Strix G16 wins on raw performance. Both are excellent — but for most gamers, the Legion 5i Pro is the smarter buy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ASUS ROG better than Lenovo Legion for gaming?

The ASUS ROG Strix G16 has the better GPU (RTX 4070 Ti Super vs RTX 4070), which means higher frame rates at 4K and better ray tracing. For 1440p gaming, both are excellent and the real-world difference is smaller than the price gap suggests. If you game at 1080p or 1440p, the Legion 5i Pro is the better value. If you push 4K or want the highest possible frame rates, the ROG wins.

Which has better thermals — ASUS ROG or Lenovo Legion?

Both handle thermals well. The ROG Strix G16 uses Tri-Fan Technology with a vapor chamber — it can get loud at max performance. The Legion 5i Pro's Coldfront 5.0 runs quieter at balanced settings. For all-day use, the Legion runs cooler and quieter. For max-performance gaming sessions, both handle the heat adequately.

Can the Lenovo Legion 5i Pro run modern AAA games?

Yes — the RTX 4070 in the Legion 5i Pro runs every current AAA title at high-to-ultra settings at 1440p. Games like Cyberpunk 2077, Hogwarts Legacy, and Black Myth: Wukong all run excellently. The RTX 4070 Ti Super in the ROG gains you 10–20 fps in the most demanding titles, which matters more at 4K than 1440p.
