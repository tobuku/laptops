Verdict

Choose the Acer Aspire 5 if: You can stretch to $479 and want a noticeably better experience. The Ryzen 7 CPU and 16GB RAM make a real difference in multitasking — running Chrome with 20 tabs, a video call, and Office without slowdown. The IPS display is also better for color accuracy. This is the right pick for students, remote workers, and anyone doing real work.

Choose the ASUS VivoBook 15 if: You need to stay under $400 and your tasks are basic — web browsing, streaming, email, and occasional Office documents. The VivoBook 15's Ryzen 5 handles light work fine. You save $80 and get the same storage capacity, just with a slower chip, less RAM, and a dimmer display.

Bottom line: For an $80 difference, the Acer Aspire 5 gives you significantly more RAM and a better display. If the budget allows, go Aspire 5. If you genuinely need to stay under $400, the VivoBook 15 is the right call.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Acer Aspire 5 better than the ASUS VivoBook 15?

Yes, for most use cases. The Aspire 5 has a faster Ryzen 7 chip and 16GB RAM vs the VivoBook's Ryzen 5 and 8GB. The VivoBook 15 is cheaper and lighter — the right choice if budget is the top priority.

Which is better for students?

The Acer Aspire 5 with 16GB RAM handles coursework more comfortably over 4 years. The VivoBook 15 covers basic student needs reliably for less money. If you're doing video editing, coding, or running multiple apps, go Aspire 5.

Can either laptop run games?

Both use integrated AMD Radeon graphics. They run esports titles at medium settings. The Aspire 5 performs slightly better due to more RAM and a faster CPU. Neither is a gaming laptop — for dedicated gaming under $500, look at the HP Victus 15 or Lenovo IdeaPad Gaming 3.
