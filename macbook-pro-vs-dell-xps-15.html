Verdict

Choose the MacBook Pro M3 Pro if: You do creative work (video editing, music production, software development), you're already in the Apple ecosystem, or you need all-day battery without exception. The M3 Pro's 22-hour battery and zero-throttle performance are unmatched in this price tier.

Choose the Dell XPS 15 if: You need Windows, you prefer a larger 15.6-inch display, or you want an OLED panel with perfect blacks. The XPS 15's 3.5K OLED touchscreen is genuinely beautiful — and at $200 less, you can upgrade to the RTX 4060 GPU option for gaming alongside creative work.

Bottom line: For creative professionals, the MacBook Pro M3 Pro is the better machine — the battery life and sustained performance advantage is real and felt every day. Windows users or anyone who needs a bigger screen should go with the XPS 15.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the MacBook Pro M3 Pro worth the extra cost over Dell XPS 15?

For macOS users and creative professionals, yes. The 9 additional hours of battery life alone changes how you work — no need to carry a charger everywhere. The M3 Pro's performance never throttles even during 4K exports. For Windows users, the extra cost doesn't make sense — the XPS 15 is the right machine.

Which is better for video editing — MacBook Pro M3 Pro or Dell XPS 15?

MacBook Pro M3 Pro by a significant margin. The M3 Pro chip includes a dedicated ProRes and ProRes RAW media engine. A 4K ProRes timeline export that takes 8 minutes on the XPS 15 takes under 3 minutes on the MacBook Pro. If you edit in Final Cut Pro, there's no contest. If you use DaVinci Resolve or Premiere, the gap is smaller but the MacBook still leads.

Does the Dell XPS 15 have a better screen than MacBook Pro?

The Dell XPS 15 OLED has perfect blacks and infinite contrast ratio — which looks stunning for movies and creative work. The MacBook Pro's Liquid Retina XDR is brighter (1000 nits vs 400 nits for the XPS OLED) and covers more of the P3 color space. For outdoor use and overall color accuracy, MacBook Pro wins. For pure OLED contrast and immersive display quality indoors, XPS 15 wins.
