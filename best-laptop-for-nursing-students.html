What Nursing Students Actually Need

Nursing school demands a lot from a laptop: ATI testing platforms, Elsevier courseware, EHR training systems, and online proctoring all need solid performance and Windows compatibility. You also need battery life that survives clinical rotations without hunting for outlets. Most nursing students don't need a $1,500 machine — a well-chosen $400–$600 Windows laptop handles everything.

Critical requirement: Confirm with your nursing program whether Windows is required before buying a MacBook. Many clinical platforms are Windows-only.

Nursing Student Laptop Buying Guide

Must-Have Requirements

  • Windows 11: Most clinical platforms (ATI, Elsevier, EHR training) require Windows. Confirm with your program.
  • 8GB RAM minimum: Online proctoring software is memory-heavy. 8GB handles it; 16GB is better if you can afford it.
  • 256GB SSD minimum: Store textbooks, recordings, and software locally. 512GB is better.
  • 8+ hour battery: Clinical rotations don't always have outlet access.
  • Webcam: Required for online proctoring (ExamSoft, Proctorio, Respondus Monitor).

Nice to Have

  • Touchscreen for annotating PDFs and digital notes
  • Under 4 lbs for carrying to clinical sites
  • Fingerprint reader for fast login during busy clinical days
