Frequently Asked Questions

How does the USB charging port on a laptop backpack work?

The USB port built into most laptop backpacks is a pass-through - it's a cable routing system, not a power source. A short internal cable connects the outside USB port to a loop inside the bag. You plug your own power bank into that internal cable, then connect your phone (or other device) to the external port. The bag itself supplies no electricity. See our Power Bank Guide for compatible picks.

What makes a laptop backpack TSA-friendly or checkpoint-friendly?

TSA-friendly (also called "checkpoint-friendly") means the laptop compartment opens completely flat - at least 180° - with no pockets, dividers, or zippers above or below the laptop layer. When placed flat on the X-ray belt, the TSA scanner gets an unobstructed view of the laptop. TSA agents can waive the requirement to remove your laptop from a bag that's certified checkpoint-friendly. Searches for this feature have grown nearly 100x year-over-year, making it one of the fastest-growing features in the category.

Are anti-theft backpacks worth it?

For urban commuters and international travelers, yes - significantly. A quality anti-theft backpack combines hidden zippers (accessible only from the back panel against your body), cut-resistant straps and lining, and RFID-blocking pockets to prevent card skimming. These aren't theoretical protections: pickpocketing and bag-slashing are genuinely common in crowded transit environments and tourist areas. The 900%+ growth in searches for anti-theft backpacks reflects that more people are treating it as a standard feature rather than a niche upgrade.

What size laptop backpack do I need for a 15.6" laptop?

Any backpack that specifies "fits up to 15.6 inch" or larger will work. The measurement refers to the diagonal screen size - the actual dimensions of your laptop will be slightly larger than the screen diagonal, so always buy for the largest screen size the backpack claims to support, not your exact screen size. If you carry a 15.6" laptop and plan to add a tablet, buy a bag that fits 17".

What's the difference between water-resistant and waterproof in a laptop backpack?

Water-resistant means the outer fabric sheds light rain and splashes - good for a commute in drizzle or setting your bag down on a wet surface. Waterproof means the bag can withstand sustained rain or submersion. Most laptop backpacks at this price range are water-resistant, not fully waterproof. If you regularly commute in heavy rain or work outdoors, look specifically for "waterproof" or "IPX" ratings, and consider a rain cover as a backup.
