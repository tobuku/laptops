The #1 Thing That Matters for Design Laptops

Display color accuracy is non-negotiable for graphic design. If your laptop's display is off by 20% in color coverage, your work will look different on other screens and in print. Look for laptops that cover 100% sRGB at minimum — 100% DCI-P3 and OLED panels are even better. After display quality, RAM (16GB+) and SSD size (1TB) matter most. You don't necessarily need an expensive dedicated GPU for 2D work.

Display Quality Guide for Designers

Color accuracy tiers from best to acceptable for design work:

  • OLED + 100% DCI-P3: Best. Infinite contrast, perfect blacks, wide gamut. Use for any professional print or brand work.
  • IPS + 100% DCI-P3: Excellent. Wide gamut without OLED contrast. Good for most design work.
  • IPS + 100% sRGB: Good. Accurate for web/screen-based design. Insufficient for print work that requires P3 coverage.
  • IPS + 72% NTSC / 45% NTSC: Avoid. Common on budget laptops. Colors will look wrong compared to most monitors and print.
