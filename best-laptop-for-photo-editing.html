What Photographers Need in a Laptop

Photo editing is demanding in specific ways: Lightroom Classic needs fast storage and plenty of RAM for its catalog, Photoshop needs RAM for large files, and both benefit from a GPU for AI-powered features. But above everything else, the display must be color-accurate — what you see on screen must match what's printed or displayed on other monitors. That's the non-negotiable.

Photo Editing Laptop Checklist

  • Display: 100% DCI-P3 or 100% sRGB minimum. OLED for the most accurate colors.
  • RAM: 16GB minimum for Lightroom. 32GB for large batch workflows.
  • Storage: 1TB SSD minimum. RAW files from full-frame cameras average 25–50MB each.
  • CPU: Modern — any AMD Ryzen 7/9 or Intel Core i7/i9 from 2022 onward handles photo editing well.
  • GPU: Not required for basic photo editing. Helpful for Photoshop's Generative Fill, Neural Filters, and 3D work.
  • Battery: 10+ hours if you shoot on location. MacBook Air M3 (18hr) is best for on-location editing.
