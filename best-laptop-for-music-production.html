What Matters for Music Production

Music production is CPU-intensive, not GPU-intensive. What matters is single-core clock speed (for real-time audio processing), RAM (for large sample libraries and virtual instruments), low DPC latency (to avoid audio glitches), and fast storage (for loading projects and samples). USB ports matter too, since most audio interfaces connect over USB-A or USB-C.

What to Look for in a Music Production Laptop

Must-Have

  • Fast multi-core CPU: Intel i7, Ryzen 7, or better. Single-core speed matters most for real-time audio processing.
  • 16GB RAM minimum: 32GB if you use large orchestral sample libraries like Kontakt or Omnisphere.
  • NVMe SSD: Samples load faster, projects open instantly. Never use a spinning hard drive for production.
  • USB-A and USB-C ports: For audio interfaces, MIDI controllers, and external drives.
  • Low DPC latency: Critical on Windows. High DPC latency causes clicks, pops, and dropouts in your audio. Check with LatencyMon.

Nice to Have

  • Thunderbolt 4: Required for pro audio interfaces like Universal Audio Apollo.
  • Fanless design: Zero noise during recording sessions. Essential if you record vocals or acoustic instruments in the same room as your laptop.
  • SD card slot: Useful for portable field recording workflows.
