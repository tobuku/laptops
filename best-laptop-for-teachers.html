What Teachers Actually Need in a Laptop

Teachers don't need the fastest processor or the best graphics card. What matters is a laptop that runs Google Classroom and Microsoft 365 smoothly, has a decent webcam for Zoom and Google Meet, is light enough to carry between classrooms, and has a battery that lasts a full school day (8+ hours minimum). A touchscreen is a bonus for annotating slides and marking up documents during presentations. Here are five laptops that check all those boxes at every budget.

Teacher Laptop Buying Guide

Must-Have Features:

  • Webcam (720p minimum, 1080p preferred for Zoom and Google Meet)
  • 8GB RAM (enough for browser tabs, Google Classroom, and video calls simultaneously)
  • 8+ hour battery (a full school day without hunting for outlets)
  • Wi-Fi 6 (schools have crowded networks, and Wi-Fi 6 handles congestion better)
  • Lightweight (under 4 lbs for carrying between rooms and to meetings)

Nice to Have:

  • Touchscreen for annotating slides and marking up documents during lessons
  • USB-A port (many school projectors and peripherals still use USB-A)
  • HDMI output for direct projector connection without needing a dongle
