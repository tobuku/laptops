Verdict

Choose the MacBook Air M3 if: You want the fastest performance in this class, the best battery life, and you're comfortable in (or moving to) the macOS ecosystem. The M3 chip makes a meaningful difference in everyday speed and compile tasks. It's also completely silent — no fan noise ever.

Choose the Surface Laptop 5 if: You need Windows, want a touchscreen for note-taking or drawing with the Surface Pen, or you're deep in the Microsoft 365 enterprise ecosystem. The Surface Laptop 5 is one of the most polished Windows ultrabooks available and costs $100 less at the same spec tier.

Bottom line: Both are excellent choices. The MacBook Air M3 wins on performance and battery. The Surface Laptop 5 wins on touch input and Windows compatibility. Let your OS preference make the decision if everything else is equal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the MacBook Air better than the Surface Laptop?

The MacBook Air M3 wins on performance — the M3 chip significantly outpaces the Intel Core i5 in the Surface Laptop 5. The Surface Laptop 5 wins on touchscreen capability and Windows integration. For pure performance and battery, the MacBook Air M3 is the better machine.

Which is better for Microsoft Office?

Both run Microsoft Office natively. The Surface Laptop 5 has touchscreen support in Office apps and tighter Windows/Microsoft 365 enterprise integration. For standalone productivity, both are equal — choose based on whether you need Windows-native features.

Does the Surface Laptop have a touchscreen?

Yes — the Surface Laptop 5 has a PixelSense touchscreen with 10-point multi-touch and Surface Pen support. The MacBook Air M3 does not have a touchscreen. If touch input matters to your workflow, the Surface Laptop wins clearly.
